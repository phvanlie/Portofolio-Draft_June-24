Checkers Game


2020

C Data Structures Memory Management Software Project Management Debugging and Testing

Checkers is a strategy game that pits two opponents against each other.
The two players, facing their 10 pieces each, battle until one of them has all their pieces captured by their opponent. The game is governed by several rules.
Moves are made diagonally. The checkerboard, the game's playing surface, indicates possible moves with alternating colored squares. A piece can only move forward, and it becomes a king when it reaches the far end of the opponent's side. Once transformed, the king gains freedom of movement and becomes more formidable. It can then move both forward and backward at will.
Some rules are not present in the game. For example, a king cannot move over multiple squares.

The 7-0 Game


2018

C Data Structures Software Project Management Decision Logic Debugging and Testing

This project involves creating the 7-0 Game.
The given statement was:
"The goal is to program the 7-0 game. In this game, multiple players take turns to play. Player A rolls two dice and adds them up. If the sum is 7, it counts as 0, and the player passes their turn. Otherwise, the player chooses either to keep this total and pass their turn or to continue, rolling again to get a partial total. They can play once, twice, three times, or even ten times. But as soon as they roll a 7, their partial total is reset to 0, and they pass their turn. The winner is the player with the most points at the end of the first round where a player reaches 200 points. Create a program that allows playing this game, either with multiple players against the computer or alone against several machine-controlled players."
